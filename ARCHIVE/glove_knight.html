Glove Diary (for the Cold-Handed Adventurer)

You have perished, do you have the fortitude to brave drain again?

You take the ticket, but this reopens painful memories of your youth when a young lass left you alone at the faire to spend time with someone else who would grow into a finer adventurer than you...

She left you with her gloves so you wouldn’t feel cold...

You sit in silence, thinking of those gloves, holding back tears for eternity....

My hands are cold, and I no longer want to live in this darkness.

Where is this glove pointing?

I would like to escape this dungeon.

I will follow this glove.

The night is beginning to dwindle.

The path of light is making its way clear to me.

This glove would make one of my hands warm.

Illuminate my path, oh glove!

Sunlight’s earnest beams pierce mine eyes.

I am unfamiliar with these translucent, blessed gloves.

Only a step away from shadow...

I must continue on my journey.

Who has defiled this glove in unsanctified rock?

For the traveller who lost it, my blessings go out to them.

Their sacrifice need not be in vain.

Perhaps the day is not the safe haven I imagined it to be...

A wonderful pair of wooly gloves sits before me.

They are filled with the hope of warmer days.

The wind is brisk and cuts at my legs, only partially covered by big shorts.

I wish I had worn my adventuring trousers for this expedition.

Do my eyes deceive me?

This must be the glove of my grandfather!

I must take a closer look at this.

A most terrible curse has been put on me, I have deceived no one but myself.

I feel compelled to say this is “dope” and give a thumbs up.

But this glove is a poor joke in my eyes.

I can no longer inhibit this vile declaration.

Will anyone free me from this spell of dishonesty?

The stones of civilization have overrun the landscape.

Is this glove a sign of hope for my freedom?

It beckons me with tantalizing, warming abandon.

I can no longer feel my bones.

I am the glove, so worn and dusty

No more to say, my tale is told

The curse on me, so very musty

Pick me up and free me, if you are bold

- C.C.
